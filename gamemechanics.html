Game Mechanics

Core Game Play

The core game play in Peblo will be a mix of puzzle solving and fighting monsters, both of which serve your progress in the game.

Fighting - The fighting in Peblo, will be slow and precise. Rather then button mashing, Peblo should be precise and smart in defeating enemies. This means utilising his surroundings and umbrella in order to destroy the enemies using timing and tactics.

I want players to pick their fights, rather then be inclined to fight every monsters. They should make a calculated decision, and weigh up whether its worth it.

Puzzles – The Puzzles in Peblo, will be difficult. I want a game which truly causes you to think, kind of like a dark souls of puzzles. Rather then being spoon fed the answer, players should feel very rewarded for coming up with a solution.

That is why its crucial that every puzzle is solvable from the get go, with a variety of solutions.

Its crucial to make the world itself a puzzle, the story of the world should be a puzzle in itself. Being solved by the narrative and by the puzzles within the game. Solving the puzzle of the world, will lead you to the end game. Rather then following a sequential pattern.

Game Flow


Macroflow

The macroflow is a progression of the challenge that adapted to the player’s skills. It is also known and related to the difficulty curve.

The goal of the macroflow is to maintain the player in the zone between anxiety and boredom. For a game designer point of view, it means managing;

  • Learning Curve

    Peblo's learning curve is linear. The learning curve, is dependant on new monsters, puzzles and player controls. The learning curve increases depending on the following factors:

    • PHYSICAL SKILLS:
      We can define the physical skills by the way the player interacts with a game controller or interact with the surface of a phone or a tablet. Those player’s skills are used in action game or arcade style game.
      • Measurement - Player must control his action using the amplitude of his movement and the level of pressure on the tumbstick/pad/buttons/stylus
      • Precision - Player must provide an accurate and precise input
      • Reflexes - The player must react to an unpredicatable stimulus and provide appropriate input at the right time
      • Timing - Player must anticipate the right moment to provide an input
      • Endurance - The player must keep doing the same action and must resist to tiredness of doing it.


    • MENTAL SKILLS:
      Mental skills were defined by the one that the player use his brain for the input. Those player’s skills are the most abstracted one and are hard to define and understand by some people.
      • Concentration - The player must retain a sequence of action for a certain amount of time. Keep Focus.
      • Management - The player must be able to organise a limited supply of scarce resources e.g. managing cool down time
      • Memory - Player must memorise and retain important information for a long period of time
      • Observation - Player must oversve an element that is part of the challenge
      • Strategy - Before jumping into action, the player must make a decision based on limited information
      • Tactics - The player must take the right decision and make the right decision by analysing obersvable information.
      • Cleverness - The player must gather information and string together clues to find a solution to a problem



  • Difficulty progression of the player

    Difficulty of progression, is based on meeting new monsters in the overworld. Learning the methods and skills to beat them. Whilst quite linear, dungeons will have difficulty curves.

Microflow

The microflow is emotionally intense. It is an experience that lasts for a short period of time but can repeat over time. If your gameplay loop doesn’t contain microflow element, there’s a big chance that your game will fail to get engage users.

HOW TO BUILD THE MICROFLOW There are 3 important means that you can integrate into your game design to help you create the microflow for your game.

  • Gameplay rythm The gameplay rythm and the player inputs are related to a temp and a rythmic pattern. Design the player inputs combination as a rythmic pattern is part of work to create the microflow. If there is no real define pattern in the player input experience, it will be harder for your game to create the microflow effect for the player.

  • The virtuous circle effect This mechanic will help the player to be and to stay in the microflow. As long as the player succeeds and stays in the flow, the challenge will become easier and stays easier.

  • Positive feedbacks and rewards The more the player succeeds in doing a specific action, the more the game should give him positive feedbacks to help to maintain him in the flow. In Candy Crush, the sugar crush sequence gives many rewards and chaining success based on the player performance. The more the player succeed to complete the puzzle in less move will make the sugar crush sequence more and more rewarding. Even so, if the level is complete, the game maintains the player in the microflow and keep him still wanting to play.

Game Play Elements

  • Umbrella

    The umbrella is a multi-purpose tool in Peblo. It can help him fight enemies, reflect projectiles, float, fly and pole vault.

  • World Seed

    The world seed is a tool to help guide the player in the game, it acts as a spiritual guide. The player doesn't know what it does, except for the fact it grows bigger whilst reaching certain points in the story.

    In actual fact its peblo's fate to become a sacrifical pawn for fate, and the world seed is just a tool at play.

Game Physics & Statistics

This is the most important section for Peblo. Peblo's core gameplay loop, relys on physics to make a dynamic and changing world.

  • Movement:

    Peblo has 2 speeds, crouching and running. As peblo grows to become more whole, he gains new abilities. Crouching being one of them. Stamina will not play an effect in Peblo. I wan't the players to instead choose their movement type based on the surrounding areas. For example crouching for when hiding from a monster. True movement will be caused by using the umbrella.

  • Flying:

    Peblo can use his umbrella to fly. Because of his weight, Peblo drops quickly meaning prolonged gliding isnt available to the player. They must instead, determine the best angle to fly to e.g. a cliff edge

  • Swimming:

    Whilst peblo cannot swim because he's to heavy. He can float using the umbrella as a boat. Peblo is unable to steer the umbrella until he acquires a special item in the mid game.

  • Rolling:

    Whilst peblo cannot swim because he's to heavy. He can float using the umbrella as a boat. Peblo is unable to steer the umbrella until he acquires a special item in the mid game.

  • Combat:

    The rate of combat depends on the method the player wishes to use to kill an enemy (if possible). Players can choose; environmental, fighting or running away.

    In order to deal damage faster, the player should understand the attack pattern of the enemy, what it' weaknesses are.

    When Peblo gets hit theres a brief period of invincilbity to allow the plaeyr to react.

Artificial Intelligence

Enemies will react based on these two basic senses, sight and sound.

  • Sight

    Enemies work on a radial system primarily. The closer, Peblo is to an enemy. The more likely he is to be found.

  • Sound

    Enemies can hear sound. Peblo can limit his sound by reducing movement or crouching. This can help in stealth like kills.
