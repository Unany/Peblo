What is a game concept?Last updated: 2019-06-01

This page describes the high-level workings of Peblo. It explains how the game is structured, how the various decisions were made, and how the programming decisions impact the game design itself. I hope this provides you with knowledge, so that your own games can excel.

The Core

The Three Liner

We have 8 seconds to capture someone's attention. Whilst you're reading this. Those second's have already gone.
That's why short and punchy descriptions are key. This is where the three liner approach comes in. If you head to the steam store page right now and click on a top seller. The majority of the time it will have three sentences in it's description.
We need to give the consumer the gist of the game, an overview of the plot and the tonality. What we really want is to get the consumer to press the video button or scroll through the images. Then hopefully, we can funnel to a sale.

"Peblo rolls you into fighting fate in this epic top-down adventure. Traverse the world to find your purpose, across a diverse and divided land. Gather your courage and propogate the land with hope!""

The game's style

For me the game's style always comes randomly. I find the time's when nothing is happening, or i'm not forcing it are the times my inspiration comes.

In the case of Peblo, I dreamt an autumn scene, walking aross the british countryside. From that inspiration I scour the web (mainly pinterest) for images that allow me to daydream scenes. It's from this daydreaming that the rest of the pieces fall together.



I then take the images and come up with a colour palette for the game. Personally, I use adobe colour wheel as it's free and allows for accompanying

Lastly, I needed to ask myself "What type of textures do I like". This was relatively easy for me, throughout my life i've adored studio ghilbi. If you've played Peblo, you'll see that the art style partially mimics this. However, In order to do this we need to take into account several things.

1. It's a handpainted style
2. The textures are simple, not overcomplicated.
3. The textures are sometimes overlayed by another texture to create depth. E.G. Moss on a rock

The game's setting

I wanted the world to be something personal, something which I have fond memories of, so that I can build the world with evocative emotion.

WHen I was younger I went on a camping trip to the lake district with my Dad and brother. I fell in love with the lake district. Rich, fond emotions are always their when I think about itself. The decision was easy! Why not make the world out of the UK, the lake district behind the setting for Peblo. Now obviously we can't copy it 1 to 1. The scale of the world would be unmangeable for a 1 person scene, so I came up with a better idea. Trace the map of the lake district.


This is a perfect stage to block out a basic world, where the rivers go, the mountains and any other features. Just remember the world WILL Change, don't spend ages doing it. I used DungeonFog for this and just blocked it out with basic colours.
